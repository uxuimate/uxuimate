Cookie Policy

Last updated: 2 May 2026 · How we use cookies and similar technologies on this site.

This policy describes cookies and similar technologies used on the UX UI MATE marketing website. It should be read together with our Privacy Policy. We keep non-essential tracking to a minimum; your browser settings remain the primary way to control storage.

1. What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make sites work more efficiently, remember preferences, or understand how pages are used. Similar technologies include local storage, session storage, and pixels.

2. Who sets cookies?

First-party cookies are set by us (or on our behalf by the same domain). Third-party cookies are set by other services that appear on our pages (for example embedded scheduling or content delivery networks).

3. Cookies we use

Strictly necessary

These are required for core site operation or security. For example, they may remember cookie consent choices where we implement a consent tool, or support load balancing. They do not require consent under UK / EEA rules because they are essential.

Functional and preferences

We may use minimal storage to remember UI choices (for example reduced motion or session-only preferences) where we add such features. These are not used for cross-site advertising.

Analytics and performance

We aim to avoid invasive third-party advertising trackers on this static site. If we add privacy-friendly analytics in future (for example aggregated page views), we will update this policy and, where required, ask for your consent before non-essential analytics run.

4. Third-party services

Parts of our site may load resources or widgets from third parties. Those providers may set their own cookies or process technical data under their policies. Examples that may apply when you use our pages include:

  • Google Fonts (fonts.googleapis.com / fonts.gstatic.com) when loading typography;
  • CDN providers (for example Cloudflare or jsDelivr) when delivering CSS or JavaScript libraries;
  • Calendly when you visit the contact page and load the scheduling widget - see Calendly's privacy policy.

We do not control third-party cookies. Please review their policies and use browser controls or opt-out tools they provide where available.

5. How to control cookies

Most browsers let you refuse or delete cookies. You can usually find these settings under "Privacy" or "Security". Blocking all cookies may affect how the site or embedded tools behave (for example scheduling).

For advertising cookies across many sites, industry tools such as Your Online Choices may help where applicable.

6. Do Not Track

There is no consistent industry standard for "Do Not Track" browser signals. We do not currently respond to DNT signals beyond our general approach of limiting non-essential tracking.

7. Changes

We may update this Cookie Policy when our site or embedded services change. The "Last updated" date above will be revised accordingly.
